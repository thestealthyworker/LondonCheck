Muslim Life in London

Better than you think — but requires active effort, not passive immersion

What London genuinely delivers

+
400+ mosques — more than any other Western city. Jumu'ah is never a logistical problem.
+
East London Mosque in Whitechapel holds 7,000+ worshippers — one of the largest in Europe.
+
Halal food is dense and diverse — South Asian, Middle Eastern, Turkish, West African. Brick Lane, Whitechapel, Green Street.
+
Areas like East Ham, Ilford, Whitechapel are effectively Muslim neighbourhoods — community is self-sustaining.
+
Large Malay-Muslim community — Singaporean and Malaysian expats, familiar cultural touchpoints.
+
Islamic schools available (state-funded and independent) — options for your boys when school age arrives.
+
Ramadan is genuinely felt in Muslim areas — communal iftars, late-night Taraweeh, Ramadan bazaars.

What London cannot give you

−
Islam is a minority religion. Your boys grow up as the exception, not the norm.
−
No adhan across the city. No ambient Islamic culture. Muslim life is sought, not absorbed.
−
Alcohol is deeply embedded in British professional culture — client dinners, work socials, EY events.
−
Islamophobia exists — not systemic but real, especially in media and political discourse.
−
Winter prayer times are brutal — Maghrib at 4pm, compressed Dhuhr/Asr in quick succession.
−
Friday prayers require deliberate planning at EY — not culturally normalised the way it is in UAE or Malaysia.

Pre-Move Action Plan

Ordered by urgency — do these before anything else

Do these NOW — before committing

Have the formal conversation with your SG employerThis week

Frame it as a fixed 24-month arrangement. Propose a remote working agreement. Get it in writing. This is the single most important pre-move step.

Clarify NHS IHS coverage with EY HRThis week

EY covers your 4 visas — but does that include the NHS Immigration Health Surcharge? GBP 1,035/person/year × 3 people × 2 years = GBP 6,210. Confirm before signing.

Push EY to increase relocation to GBP 10–12KBefore signing

GBP 4K for a family of 4 is below EY's own standard. Reference EY's global mobility policy. Ask for itemised relocation support. Worst case they say no.

Do these before you leave Singapore

Get HDB approval to rent out your flat2 months out

Apply to HDB for approval to sublet while overseas. Rental income SGD 2,500–3,500/mo is a meaningful additional buffer.

Set CPF OA to cash mortgage payments1 month out

If CPF OA funds the mortgage, switch to cash payments or ensure OA balance remains sufficient. You cannot contribute to CPF from overseas employment.

Consult a Singapore tax advisor1 month out

IRAS may still tax your SG-sourced income even if earned in London. A one-time consultation prevents a costly surprise.

Register Scrypt as a UK limited companyFirst month in London

Gives you professional identity in the UK from day 1. Simple Companies House registration. Lets you invoice UK clients cleanly.

Open a UK bank account (Starling or Monzo)Before arrival

Apply before arriving if possible. Having a UK account ready speeds up rental deposits and salary receipt.
